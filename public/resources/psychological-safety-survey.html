Psychological Safety Survey

A Comprehensive Team Health Assessment
by Vishal

About This Survey

This survey is designed to assess psychological safety within your engineering team. Psychological safety is the belief that you can speak up without risk of punishment or humiliation.

Use this survey every 6 months to track team health, identify areas for improvement, and ensure your team feels safe to take risks, ask questions, and voice concerns.

Target Audience: Engineering managers conducting team assessments for direct and indirect reports.

Response Scale

For each question, use a 5-point scale:

  • 1 - Strongly Disagree - This never happens or is completely untrue
  • 2 - Disagree - This rarely happens or is mostly untrue
  • 3 - Neutral - This sometimes happens or is partially true
  • 4 - Agree - This often happens or is mostly true
  • 5 - Strongly Agree - This always happens or is completely true

1. Speaking Up & Voice

  • I feel comfortable asking questions when I don't understand something
  • I can voice my opinions and concerns without fear of negative consequences
  • I feel safe to admit when I've made a mistake
  • I can disagree with my manager or team members without fear of retaliation
  • I feel comfortable suggesting new ideas or approaches
  • I can ask for help when I need it without feeling judged
  • I feel safe to challenge the status quo or existing processes
  • I can express my concerns about project direction or decisions

2. Learning & Growth

  • I feel comfortable taking on new challenges and learning opportunities
  • I can experiment with new approaches without fear of failure
  • I feel supported when trying to learn new skills or technologies
  • I can ask for feedback without feeling defensive
  • I feel safe to share my learning goals and development needs
  • I can participate in knowledge sharing sessions without feeling judged
  • I feel comfortable mentoring others or being mentored
  • I can admit when I don't know something and need to learn

3. Team Collaboration

  • I feel comfortable collaborating with all team members
  • I can share my work-in-progress without fear of criticism
  • I feel safe to ask for code reviews and feedback
  • I can participate in team discussions without feeling excluded
  • I feel comfortable working with team members from different backgrounds
  • I can contribute to team decisions without feeling ignored
  • I feel safe to express different perspectives in team meetings
  • I can work effectively with team members who have different working styles

4. Error Handling & Failure

  • I feel safe to report bugs or issues I've introduced
  • I can discuss failures and what went wrong without blame
  • I feel comfortable participating in post-mortems and retrospectives
  • I can admit when I need more time or resources for a project
  • I feel safe to raise concerns about project risks or potential issues
  • I can discuss technical debt and quality concerns openly
  • I feel comfortable flagging when something doesn't feel right
  • I can share lessons learned from mistakes without fear of judgment

5. Management & Leadership

  • I feel comfortable giving feedback to my manager
  • I can discuss my career goals and aspirations openly
  • I feel safe to express concerns about workload or work-life balance
  • I can ask for clarification on expectations without feeling inadequate
  • I feel comfortable discussing compensation and promotion opportunities
  • I can voice concerns about team dynamics or interpersonal issues
  • I feel safe to suggest improvements to team processes or culture
  • I can discuss my strengths and areas for improvement honestly

6. Inclusion & Belonging

  • I feel like I belong on this team
  • I can be my authentic self at work
  • I feel valued for my unique contributions and perspectives
  • I can participate in team social activities without feeling excluded
  • I feel comfortable sharing personal information when relevant
  • I can express my cultural or personal values without judgment
  • I feel safe to discuss diversity and inclusion topics
  • I can advocate for others without fear of negative consequences

7. Open-Ended Questions

  • What makes you feel most safe and supported on this team?
  • What situations or behaviors make you feel unsafe or uncomfortable?
  • How could we improve psychological safety on our team?
  • What would help you feel more comfortable speaking up?
  • Are there any specific incidents that affected your sense of safety?
  • What support do you need from your manager to feel more psychologically safe?
  • How do you think we could better handle mistakes and failures?
  • What changes would make you feel more included and valued?

Note: These open-ended questions should be answered in writing to provide detailed insights and specific examples.

Best Practices for Conducting This Survey

  1. Ensure Anonymity: Use anonymous survey tools and never try to identify individual responses
  2. Communicate Purpose: Explain why you're conducting the survey and how results will be used
  3. Set Expectations: Let team members know this is about improvement, not evaluation
  4. Follow Up: Share results with the team and create action plans based on findings
  5. Regular Cadence: Conduct this survey every 6 months to track progress over time
  6. Act on Results: Use survey data to make concrete changes and improvements
  7. Protect Confidentiality: Never share individual responses or use them against team members
  8. Create Safe Space: Ensure team members feel comfortable being honest in their responses

Scoring & Analysis

How to Score and Interpret Results

Overall Team Score: Calculate the average score across all questions (1-5 scale)

  • 4.0-5.0: Excellent psychological safety - team is thriving
  • 3.0-3.9: Good psychological safety - some areas for improvement
  • 2.0-2.9: Moderate psychological safety - significant improvements needed
  • 1.0-1.9: Poor psychological safety - immediate action required

Category Analysis: Look for patterns in specific areas (speaking up, learning, collaboration, etc.)

Trend Analysis: Compare results over time to measure improvement

Action Planning: Focus on the lowest-scoring areas for targeted improvements
